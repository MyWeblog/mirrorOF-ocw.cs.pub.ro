October 2, 2016: Welcome to the wonderful and challenging world of security. Put on your hackers hats, get your assembly skills on, start thinking in binary and let's get cracking! The Computer and Network Security class focuses on offensive security: i.e. reverse engineering, binary analysis, exploiting and runtime application security. Starting from this year we will not tackle crypto or web security as these are going to be better handled at other classes. Enjoy the ride!

The first Computer and Network Security lecture will take place on Monday, October 3, 2016, room PRECIS 106. The first Computer and Network Security lab will take place on Tuesday, October 4, 2016, 6pm-8pm, room PRECIS 706, followed by the next one in the next timeslot (Tuesday, October 4, 2016, 8pm-10pm, room PRECIS 706).

Please use the Lab Split page for lab sessions splitting.

For starters please join the mailing list. The administrative staff will also provide you access to the cs.curs.pub.ro class.
